IP Korean Beef

4 servings780 cal • 50g P • 70g C • 20g F
⌄
Ingredients
  • 800g braising beef (chuck), cut into 3cm chunks
  • 3 pak choi, halved lengthways
  • 5 tbsp soy sauce (low sodium if possible)
  • 3 tbsp gochujang paste
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 250ml beef stock (½ stock cube + water)
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2cm fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes (optional)
  • 4 spring onions + sesame seeds to serve
  • 300g brown rice (prep separately in IP)
Method
  1. Whisk all sauce ingredients (soy, gochujang, brown sugar, stock, garlic, ginger, sesame oil, rice vinegar, chilli) in a bowl
  2. Place beef chunks in IP. Pour sauce over and toss to coat
  3. HIGH PRESSURE 35 minutes + 10 min natural release
  4. SAUTE mode: simmer 5 min to reduce sauce slightly
  5. Steam pak choi: microwave with a splash of water 2–3 min
  6. Serve beef over rice with pak choi. Garnish with spring onions and sesame seeds

Batch Chicken Thighs (IP)

IP 25 min HP + 10 min NRMakes ~1.2kg shredded
⌄
Ingredients
  • 2kg boneless, skinless chicken thighs (defrosted overnight from frozen)
  • 500ml chicken stock (1 stock cube + water)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper
Method
  1. Add stock to Instant Pot
  2. Season chicken with garlic powder, paprika, salt and pepper
  3. Place chicken in IP on trivet or directly in stock
  4. HIGH PRESSURE 25 minutes + 10 min natural release
  5. Remove chicken, shred with two forks — discard liquid
  6. Split into two equal portions (~600g each): one for Teriyaki Noodles, one for Chicken Curry

Salmon & Chickpea Coconut Curry

4 servings820 cal • 48g P • 82g C • 28g F
⌄
Ingredients
  • 800g salmon fillets, skin off, cut into chunks
  • 2 × 400g tins chickpeas, drained
  • 2 × 400ml tins full-fat coconut milk
  • 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 200g baby spinach
  • 2 red peppers, sliced
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 thumb fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 300g brown rice (prep separately in IP)
Method
  1. SAUTE mode: heat oil, fry onion 3 min until softened
  2. Add garlic, ginger, curry powder, cumin, coriander — stir 1 min
  3. Add chopped tomatoes and deglaze the pot
  4. Add coconut milk, chickpeas, red peppers, salt and pepper — stir
  5. Lay salmon chunks on top — do not stir
  6. HIGH PRESSURE 4 minutes + quick release
  7. Open lid, stir gently — salmon will break into flakes naturally
  8. Stir through spinach until wilted. Add garam masala and lemon juice.
  9. Serve over rice

Beef & Sweet Potato Stew

4 servings880 cal • 55g P • 86g C • 22g F
⌄
Ingredients
  • 800g diced braising beef
  • 2 large sweet potatoes (~600g), peeled and cubed
  • 200g baby spinach
  • 3 carrots, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 600ml beef stock (2 stock cubes + water)
  • 1 × 400g tin chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 tbsp cornflour (mixed with 2 tbsp water)
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 300g brown rice (prep separately in IP)
Method
  1. SAUTE mode: heat oil, brown beef in batches 3–4 min — set aside
  2. Fry onion 3 min. Add garlic, tomato puree, paprika, thyme — stir 1 min.
  3. Add beef stock and deglaze. Add chopped tomatoes.
  4. Return beef. Add carrots and sweet potato.
  5. HIGH PRESSURE 35 minutes + 10 min natural release
  6. Stir through spinach until wilted
  7. If sauce too thin: SAUTE mode, stir in cornflour slurry, simmer 2 min
  8. Serve over rice

Garlic & Ginger Prawn Stir Fry

4 servings680 cal • 44g P • 68g C • 12g F
⌄
Ingredients
  • 800g raw king prawns (defrost in cold water 30 min)
  • 2 heads broccoli, cut into small florets
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 2 pak choi, leaves separated
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3cm fresh ginger, grated
  • 4 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp chilli flakes
  • 1 tsp cornflour mixed with 2 tbsp cold water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 4 spring onions + sesame seeds to serve
  • 300g brown rice (prep separately in IP)
Method
  1. Mix sauce: soy, oyster sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, chilli flakes. Set aside
  2. Pat prawns dry with kitchen paper
  3. Heat wok until smoking. Add vegetable oil
  4. Stir fry broccoli and carrots 3–4 min until just tender with some char. Set aside
  5. Add garlic and ginger to wok — stir 30 sec (do not burn)
  6. Add prawns — stir fry 2–3 min until pink. Do not overcook
  7. Return veg. Add pak choi, toss 1 min
  8. Pour sauce over. Add cornflour slurry, stir until slightly thickened
  9. Serve over rice. Garnish with spring onions and sesame seeds. Vincent: add extra rice.

Teriyaki Chicken Noodles

4 servings760 cal • 52g P • 82g C • 18g F
⌄
Ingredients
  • 600g shredded chicken (from batch)
  • 300g medium egg noodles (dry)
  • 2 heads broccoli, cut into florets
  • 4 carrots, julienned
  • 4 spring onions, sliced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 thumb fresh ginger, grated
  • 6 tbsp mirin
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce (light)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • Salt and pepper
Method
  1. Cook noodles per packet — drain and set aside
  2. Mix sauce: mirin, soy sauce, rice vinegar, 1 tsp sesame oil
  3. High heat wok: heat vegetable oil, stir-fry broccoli and carrots 3–4 min
  4. Add garlic and ginger — stir 30 sec
  5. Add shredded chicken — toss to heat through
  6. Add noodles and pour over sauce — toss everything together 2 min
  7. Finish with remaining sesame oil and spring onions. Vincent: add extra noodles to hit calorie target.

IP Asian Braised Mackerel

4 servings760 cal • 52g P • 80g C • 22g F
⌄
Ingredients
  • 4 × 125g tins mackerel in brine, drained
  • 2 × 400g tins chopped tomatoes
  • 200g baby spinach
  • 200g fine green beans
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
